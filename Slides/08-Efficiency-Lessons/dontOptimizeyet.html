The second rule: Don't Yet

Even if a program you're writing will eventually need some optimization, you probably won't know which parts of it will need to be optimized until you've written the program and had a chance to measure its performance on realistic test data.

When you begin to measure the performance of a program, you are likely to find that some parts of the program are already adequately efficient.

You may also find that other parts of the program, which you thought would be adequately efficient, turn out to be where the program is spending most of its time.

Optimization should be guided by actual measurements and profiling. If you're optimizing a program before you know where the program is spending its time, you are probably wasting your time.

Even if a program you're writing will eventually need some optimization, you need to remember the second rule: Don't yet.
