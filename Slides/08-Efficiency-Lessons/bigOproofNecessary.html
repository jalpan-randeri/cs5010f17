The Hypotheses Are Necessary

In the second theorem, the hypothesis that says (λ n . 1) ∈ O(f) is annoying but necessary.

Consider the following functions:

f(n) = min (1, 1/n)

g(n) = 1

Then g ∈ O(f) by the more general definition, but g ∉ O(f) by the less general definition.

That means the less general definition is not exactly the same as the definition we'd get by restricting the more general definition to functions that accept only natural numbers as arguments.

In computer science, the less general definition of O(f) is almost never used unless f is positive-valued and monotonic, which implies   (λ n . 1) ∈ O(f). That's why, in computer science, it is almost always safe to use the more general definition instead of the less general.
