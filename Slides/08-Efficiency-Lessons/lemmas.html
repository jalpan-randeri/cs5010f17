Useful Lemmas

Lemma. For any real constants k1 and k2 with k1 positive,

O(f) = O(λ x . k1 f(x) + k2).

Lemma. If f1 and f2 are real polynomials of one real variable, and their leading coefficients are positive, and they have the same degree, and both are restricted to non-negative real arguments, then O(f1) = O(f2).

Lemma. If f1 and f2 are polynomials as described in the previous lemma except the degree of f1 is less than the degree of f2, then O(f1) is a proper subset of O(f2). In particular, f2 is not an element of O(f1).

Lemma. O(ln x) = O(lg x) = O(logb x).

Lemma. When each function is restricted to non-negative real arguments,

O(1) ⊂ O(lg x) ⊂ O(x) ⊂ O(x lg x)

⊂ O(x2) ⊂ O(x3) ⊂ ...

⊂ O(2x) ⊂ O(exp(x)) ⊂ (3x) ⊂ ...

For each lemma above, an analogous lemma holds for Ω in which the subset inequalities are reversed.
