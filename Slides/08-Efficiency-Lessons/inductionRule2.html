Having proved IH(0), we now need to prove the second antecedent of the inference rule for induction:

(∀ n) if IH(n) then IH(n+1)

That is a universally quantified statement. To prove it, we have to show it holds for all n. To prove that, we have to show

if IH(n) then IH(n+1)

is true for any natural number n.

To prove a statement of the form if X then Y, we assume X is true and prove Y follows by the rules of logic, mathematics, and computer science.

And so, in our proof of correctness for quicksort, we proceed as follows:

  • We let n be an arbitrary natural number.
  • We assume IH(n), which says quicksort returns the correct result on all lists whose length is less than or equal to n.
  • We then try to prove IH(n+1): quicksort returns the correct result on all lists whose length is less than or equal to n+1.
