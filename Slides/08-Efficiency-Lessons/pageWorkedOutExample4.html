We think c0 = 40 and c1 = 2 will work. Substituting for those constants and for f1(x) and f2(x), we get

f1 (x) ≤ c0 + c1 * f2 (x)

3 x + 30 ≤ 40 + 2 * (x (x + 1) / 2)

which simplifies to

0 ≤ 10 - 2 x + x2

That's the inequality we must prove.

Now we see why this is so hard. The minus sign is working against us.
